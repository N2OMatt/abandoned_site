Intro:

This week I had a friendly debate on work with my colleagues about the footprint that you leave on internet and how it can affect you in the view of the recruiters.

I'll try in this post expose my views about the subject and with a fortunately persuade you to do the same! So without any further ado, let's start!

The discussion:

It started as I tell them one inbox message that I received on LinkedIn by one of my connections that - I think in the best of intentions - advised me to not expose my ideas as I did.

To contextualize, my post was:

The guy that replied me said that I shouldn't do that, since I'll be ill viewed by the recruiters and all that stuff...
Firstly, I said thanks to person that take care of me to the extend to let me a message warning about it. I mean, while I disagree with him, I'm most pleased to know that he wants the best for me and sincerely took some time to alert me.

Well I did exposed the same situation for my fellow coworkers and most of them agreed with the message's guy. They said that, ..."yes, we should take care about we post, not only on LinkedIn, but on other social networks as well..." and ..."no matter how good you're on the tech side, some recruiters aren't or don't care about it at first glance, and you might lose a job opportunity by doing that"...

Again, I thanks by the alert (for real), but I strongly disagree with all of them, so let me make my point...

Why this matter:

Let's say that you started your career as professional by the 20 years old, and plan to retire on the 55 years old.
That's 35 years of work, assuming a journey of (A)8 hours a day, (B)5 days by week - and not considering for this count the vacation time, holidays and that stuff - you gonna work:

  • 1 year = ~52 weeks
  • 52 weeks/year * 35 years of work = 1825 weeks
  • 1825 weeks * 5 days/week = 9125 days of work
  • 9125 days * 8 hours of work/day = 73.000 hours
  • 73.000 hours of work / 24 hours (of entire day) = ~3041 days
  • ~3041 days / 365 days/year = ~8.3 years
That's it, you gonna work on your life:
  • 9125 days
  • 73.000 hours
  • ~8.3 years working 24 hours per day
I really don't know for you, but for me that's a fucking long time.
This is a great part of the life, I mean GREAT part of it!

By this calculation I just working with the raw numbers, I don't taking in account that when you are working, you're not - in the most common case - with the people that you love, you're not enjoying you dog, you're not watching you kids grow, etc.
See what I mean? If the hours aren't bad enough, when you are almost obligated to work to have a live in the modern society, you're being forced to not only spend a lot of time on it, but be apart of a lot of key points of your life.

This led us to what I decided to do...

I give a shit about recruiters:

Yes, I really give a shit...

Addendum: I really repect the profession and the people that do recruitment. What I mean here, is I don't care and won't do nothing to make me more likeable to them if by doing that I would go against myself.

I wasn't always being like that, actually I was kind of opposite, trying to do everything "right" to be better in their eyes. But when I realized that I had no option in the modern society to live without a job, and that a job takes a enormous amount of my time, I decided that I must prioritize some things, and they are:

  1. I'm the most important "thing" on the world, exactly as you're!
  2. Work is great but comes in expense a lot of great things too
So by (1) I know that I must do everything thing that makes me happy while ensuring that I make all people happy too. I mustn't do anything that in conflict with myself just o let someone have a better impression on me. Please notice that you're responsible in some degree for the happiness of the other people too, and you shall not do anything that may harm them.
And by (2) I'm forced to exchange some key points of my life, so I'll try everything to have the better of the work - because I know that this is inevitable.


While (1) is very powerful the (2) led to my point here:

If recruiters don't like what I say or do, this is a bless! if they thinks that I don't fit to the company, I'm sure that the company doesn't FITS me!.
As I said, I'll spend a hell lot of time there, I want to be myself - remember (1)?? - I want be connected with good people that, while they might think differently, have the knowledge that the person is the most import thing.

Conclusions:

I won't be different just to please the business - They give a fuck for the individuals anyway. I won't spend my life, myself, my essence to let someone richer.
I'll be myself, strive hard to be happy and make people happy!

And if by doing that I lose some job opportunities, well I don't see as I lose a job, but instead as I won more opportunities to be happy!
