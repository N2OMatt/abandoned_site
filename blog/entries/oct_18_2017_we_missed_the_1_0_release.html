Today is: Tuesday 18, October of 2017 - Curitiba, Paraná.

Intro:

Summer time already began and with a hour less of sleep out week starts!
Great (but not)! I'm still in Curitiba, since we failed to deliver the 1.0 release.

Today I'll try to be mindful about what went wrong and what we need to do to not fail next deadline.

What failed:

Well, we have a not too complex system, but yet having some challenges about it. I mean, the programming part is almost trivial (with some caveats listed bellow), but the business part is very troublesome.

The mis-understanding about the business part of the application led to a situation where, in some critical parts of the application, no one understand what's to implement and/or how it should be implemented.

This led to a situation where, a day before release (When I said day, you can safely read - Some hours before) some mis-interpretations about what a specific part of program should do, popped out.
And as all we know this can only mean on thing: A complete disaster!

The result was a sub-optimal product that we decide to not release yet. This was a wise decision since, by the time that the product is release we must take reposability for what we delivered.

So this week is being very busy, fixing all the bugs that we found along the way and trying to make the code more robust and stable at same time.
I know, we're doing it wrong, but it's what we have for now...

Why we failed:

  • The technology chosen was sub-optimal for what we're doing currently.
    • We're fast moving and while we have a big picture of what we're trying to achieve, the minor details (and they did show that they count up) are very hidden.
    A relational database with a language with a slow development turn around is very sub-optimal in my option.
    The best option is using a NoSQL database with a language that's more fit to a rapid development like python or ruby.
  • Too many key people changes...
    • In a period of 3 or 4 months the project changed direction 3 times. This was due the architects being fired by not deliver what was expected from them.
    Needless to say that I face a very difficult challenge where I have to design the system built upon a very fragmented and weak structure.
  • Lack of testers...
    • The majority of the bugs aren't, to say, code bugs, but domain bugs. Summed up with the lack of a user "testing" the application to ensure that it's what the client wants, makes everything really error prone.
  • Not everyone is playing by the same rules!
    • This is the worst thing that can happen in a team and unfortunately we faced it. Some people just hidden information about the status of their work and at the last corner of at last lap it was shown.

Next Steps:

We have so many challenges and issues to overcome, that's really hard to enumerate them all, but we can more or less split them in two sets of problems: Short-term problems (That needs to be addressed quickly to next deliver):

  1. Close all bugs possible until the end of week.
  2. Test every change against what we think that user wants
  3. Write down all the key points that will be need to be refactored
Long-term problems (Stuff that we need address after we finish this release):
  1. A more clear division of the work.
    2. Today we don't have a clear separation of each person responsibilities.
    So as expected this leads to the situation that some stuff is neglect and other stuff generates a lot of friction between the members of the team.
  2. A stop and REFACTOR
    3. The project will suffer and probably will be dead really soon if we won't stop and rethink its design and implementation.
  3. Move towards a mindset of GET THE SHIT DONE
    4. Today we're really verbose, too much stupid and needless meetings to reach nothing. Would be great define what's to be achieve and just do it®.
